hat to bring

In 4 of the 10 weeks we are 4 persons aboard and have to be really critical about what to bring for weight reasons. However, we have arranged it such that we are flying along the east- and north-east cost in this period, and therefore the nearest aerodrome will never be far away. Hence we don't have to bring full fuel.

Below you find a list of what we need to bring. Anything missing? Anything superfluous?



Essential stuff for the plane, flying, navigation, safety
  • Extra water
  • ELT (Emergency Locator Transmitter)
  • Mobile phone
  • Maps (WAC, TAC, WTC, AEL, tourist map)
  • Aviation books (ERSA, VFR Flight Guide, logbook, Airfield Directory 2000, Country Airstrip Guide for WA)
  • Pens
  • 4 set of headphones
  • Earplugs
  • Chocks
  • Life wests for flying across Bass stait (to hire in Melborne)


Essentials for traveling/touristing (shared)
  • Drinks & snacks
  • Laptop
  • PalmPilot
  • Camera + extra film and batteries
  • Lonely Planet Australia
  • Binoculars
  • Insect repellent
  • Sun screen
  • Shampoo, soap and tooth paste
  • Matches / lighter
  • Torch
  • Swiss army knife
  • Washing powder
  • Tea and coffee (bags)
  • Salt & Pepper


Personal stuff (per person)
  • Towel
  • Bathers
  • Toilet stuff
  • Good shoes for walking
  • Sandals
  • 2 pair of shorts
  • 4 t-shirts
  • 1 pair of trowsers
  • 1 warm sweather
  • 1 wind/rain jacket
  • Socks, undies
  • Sunnies
  • Cap
  • Small backpack


Extra, when we are only two persons
  • Tent
  • 2 sleeping bags & mats
  • Kellogg's Special K and milk :-)
  • Cooling bag and freezer block
  • Camping plates, cups & cutlery


Possibly
  • Extra GPS
  • Country Airstrip Guide for other states
