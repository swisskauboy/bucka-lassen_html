Flying with Klaus

Things to bring

  • Camera / video camera / GoPro / etc.
  • Something to drink / snack (there is no restaurant at the airport) / the on-board stewardess has quit due to bad working conditions, she said
  • Not so tall people should consider bringing a pillow they can sit on - especially when they will be sitting in the co-pilots seat in front

Things to do and know

  • Please be on time (as agreed). The plane is reserved only for a specific block of time and there are certain other constraints that might mean we can't go flying at all, should you arrive late.
  • Remember to go to the toilet before we depart (I will try to remember to remind you of this, but should I forget you have been warned ;-)
  • Send Klaus a list of the passengers and their weights in advance. He needs this to do some calculations regarding take-off and landing distance which are influenced by the total weight of the plane - important for safety. Don't bring heavy things you don't need in the plane
  • For Switzerland 330 per hour (or rather 5.50/minute) is quite inexpensive for a 4-seater plane (pilot + 3 passengers). The more passengers, the lower the price per person. With three passengers, everybody only pays about CHF 1.85 per minute or CHF 55 for the shortes possible flight of 30 minutes.

Where to go

  • We can go anywhere (almost) - the choice is yours and the sky is the limit ;-). Just give Klaus a list of place (preferably with GPS coordinates) of places you would like to see from above. This could be your house, your work, childhood memories, your favorite hiking or skiing area, or just about anything that you imagine would look cool from above.
  • We don't have to plan up front where we go, we can also make up where we go (and we can to some extent also change our minds) once we are in the air
  • However, some places lie within regulated airspaces (like most of the city of Z rich for instance) and if we want to fly over those spots, I will have to file a flight plan up front (need to know at least 24 hours before)
  • Some standard routes I have done a lot of times over the years are (click map below to se details)

How to get to the airport

  • Please note that some navigation systems might guide you to the wrong side (north) of the airport. Please study the map first and make sure you arrive at the correct place on the southern side of the runway.
  • There is a "Do not enter" sign at the turn of from the main road (about halfways between Rifferswil and Kappel am Albis) towards the airport - ignore this and continue to the parking at the airport.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Klaus, is this your own plane? => No, it isn't. I'm a member of a club where plane owners make their bird available. I'm just renting this (and other) planes
  • Does it cost extra to land somewhere halfways? => No, it doesn't. Well, there is a landing fee that is to be paid, but I (Klaus) pay that, so it won't cost extra. Landing and starting though probably adds about 5 minutes to the flight, compared to if we just flew over the airstrip instead without landing there. While the plane is on the ground though, it doesn't cost anything. You only pay for time in the air (not even time the engine is running, as long as it is on ground).
  • What kind of plane are we flying? => It is a low-winged American built Piper Archer II seating the pilot (me) and three passengers; registration HB-PGM
  • What is the price per passenger? => Well, that depends on how many passengers there are. With only one passenger, it's CHF 330 per hour (= 5.50/minute); with two passengers, it's CHF 165 per person per hour (= 2.75/minute); with three passengers, it's CHF 110 per person per hour (= 1.85/minute).
  • You want my weight, but I'm not comfortable sharing that, what do I do? => There is no way around it, for safety reasons and by law, I have to do a weight & balance calculation. I won't share your weight with others, of course. The maximum the three passengers may weigh together is approx. 260 kg.
  • How can we best prepare for this flight? => Have a think about who is going to sit in the front. It is the best of the passenger seats, so the person taking that seat could also be asked to pay a bit extra. Say CHF 2.00/minute while the rear seat passengers both pay 1.75/minute for a total of the 5.50/minute.
  • What influence does the weather have? Weather is everything! Sometimes we're lucky and we have perfect weather for flying. Few clouds, no turbulence, not too hot. At other times the decision is also easy to no go flying because of far too many clouds, rain or heavy winds. And quite often we will be confronted with something in between. Some clouds which allow us to get airborn but also will prevent us from flying a strict route we might have planed for. Then we have to "wing it" and fly what and where we ca. OR, we cancel the flight and go at another time. I'm only certified for flying VFR (Visual Flight Rules), which means, I can't fly through clouds.
  • When do we decide on whether we go flying or not? This is all dependent on the weather. Sometimes we can make a definite decision already one or even two days before. Mostly though, we will leave the decision to as late as possible, which generally means on the day itself. If you, for whatever reason, need to know the decsion earlier, let Klaus know. Even when we already are in the air, there might be circumstances that mean Klaus will turn around and call it of. Safety first. Weather forecasts are just that, forecasts - nothing else.
  • Is the pilot paying for the rental of the plane as well? Yes, he is. CHF 330/h isn't the full price. There is a yearly club fee, landing fees, license and medical fees, plus a whole lot of other expenses for maps, apps, weather subscriptions, etc. Your pilot is covering all of that. The total price for the plane, everything included, is probably closer to somewhere between CHF 400/h and CHF 450/h.
