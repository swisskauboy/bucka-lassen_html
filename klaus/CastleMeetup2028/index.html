Getting there and parking

You can unload your car near the castle (or even right in front of the castle if you have a small 4x4), but you can't park there. The free parking lot is about 10 minutes walk away.

If you arrive by public transport, take the train to Thusis and

  • ... catch a Taxi from the train station
  • ... get somebody who already is at the castle to pick you up
  • ... walk (but it is a 45 min. walk up the hill)
  • ... take the bus B513 or B521 to Sils im Domleschg (5 min) and walk from there (15 min)
Check train and bus connections here at SBB (Swiss Railway)

Packing List (for Knights, Ladies, and Squires of All Ages)

✅ Essentials

  • Towel (unless you want to drip-dry)
  • Torch/flashlight (for midnight bathroom quests and late-night ghost hunts)
  • Slippers or indoor shoes (stone floors and cold toes are a bad combo)
  • Water bottle (stay hydrated, even medieval-style)

🧴 Toiletries

  • Toothbrush, toothpaste
  • Earplugs (yes, some snore like angry ogres)

🧥 Clothing

  • Warm jumper or fleece (castles are not known for central heating)
  • Hiking gear: boots, daypack
  • Rain gear (just in case the heavens open)
  • Swimsuit (a must—mountain lakes, rivers, and spontaneous hose fights await!)
  • Costumes for medieval mischief (optional but very encouraged)

🎲 Extras

  • Games (board, card, etc.)
  • Musical instruments (lute, triangle, ukulele, war drum… we’re not picky)
  • Snacks & secret chocolate stash
  • Phone charger (plus power bank if you’re planning to roam far)

🛏️ What NOT to bring

  • Bedding (linen and stuff) – already provided by the castle staff!

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is there Wi-Fi at the castle?
A: Sometimes - don't count on it. The real network is human anyway.

Q: Can we cook in the castle?
A: Absolutely! There’s a really good kitchen on-site, complete with a semi-professional dishwasher. Perfect for preparing royal feasts and post-hike hunger attacks.

Q: Will I get a private room?
A: No, noble guest, you will not. Bring a night gown and a sense of humor.

Q: What about laundry?
A: Let’s just say... pack enough. Or get creative with the river.

Q: Will there be a daily schedule?
A: Sort of.

Q: I don’t know anyone!
A: You will. Quickly. And probably for life. That’s the whole point.

The Ehrenfels Code of Chivalry

  1. Bring curiosity. Leave titles, roles, and egos at the drawbridge.
  2. Contribute what you can. Organize a walk, stir a pot, lead a silly game. No act too small.
  3. Respect quiet hours. Approx. 23:00 to 07:30 (unless a dragon attacks).
  4. Honor the Cleanup Code. Leave things better than you found them.
  5. Include everyone. We're many nations, many ages, many backgrounds. That’s our magic.
  6. Capture memories, not just selfies. Photos are great. So is being fully present.
  7. Signal will again be our messenger hawk.
  8. We laugh with, not at. Unless it’s Klaus in tights. Then, fair game.

Other information (relevant for planning your stay)

Stay

Hotel Options

If you're into having your own room and shower or are planning a shorter stay than 6 nights, consider booking at one of the following nearby hotels:

  • Sils im Domleschg (15 minutes by foot to the castle, easy hike, 70 meters altitude gain)
    • Gasthaus Post – the primary hotel option in Sils. I couldn't find it on booking.com, so you probably have to book directly with them.
  • Thusis (30-45 minutes by foot to the castle, a hike, 60 meters altitude gain)
    • Nolla Apartment – Exceptional ratings (9.6 on booking.com), great for a more private stay.
    • Gyger AG Restaurant Confiserie B&B – Highly rated (8.7) and budget-friendly.
    • Story Thusis – Superb ratings (9.0), higher-end option. This is also where we rented the bikes in 2025.
    • Hotel Weiss Kreuz – Very good ratings (8.5), with breakfast included.

If you’re thinking of staying in a hotel, I suggest booking early to ensure availability. You're welcome to have breakfast with the rest of us in the castle. Typically this is between 8 and 10 am.

Staying in the Castle

If you'd like to stay in the castle itself, please let me know as soon as possible so I can reserve your spot. When not booked for events like ours here, the castle operates as a youth hostel. It has 35 beds (mostly bunks) available across 6 dorm rooms (with 12, 8, 6, 4, 3, and 2 beds). Preference for staying in the castle will be given to those staying the full 6 nights.

The total price for 6 nights in the castle will probably be around CHF / EUR / USD 200. Included are a pillow, duvet and linnen. Towel you have to bring yourself or rent for CHF 3.

If you book a spot in the castle, don't pay just yet. You'll receive an e-mail with paying instruction later. Should you need to cancel after payment, and we can find a substitute (which is quite likely), you'll receive a full refund, of course.

And yes, I know, everybody wants the 2- or 3-bed rooms, but unfortunately, that's not possible ;-). So only ask for one if your really have a good reason
